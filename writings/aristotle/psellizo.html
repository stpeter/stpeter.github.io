ψελλίζω in Aristotle

translated by Peter Saint-Andre

The following passages constitute occurrences of the term ψελλίζω in the works of Aristotle.

  • 328b9 ψελλίζεται = attempting to combine materials like tin and bronze shows that some things are "hesitant" [ψελλίζεται] and "ambivalent" [ἐπαμφοτερίζει] towards each other
  • 536b8 ψελλίζουσι = children "babble" when they are too young to have control over their tongues
  • 660a26 ψελλίζονται = the speech of those who are tongue-tied is "babbling"
  • 898b34 ψελλίζομεν = for a long time after birth we merely "babble"
  • 985a5 ψελλίζεται = the "babbling" expressions of Empedocles
  • 993a15 ψελλιζομένῃ = the earliest philosophy was like the speech of one who "babbles"
