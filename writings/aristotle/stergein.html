Aristotle on στέργειν

translated by Peter Saint-Andre

The following passages constitute the occurrences of the term στέργειν (and στοργή) in the works of Aristotle.

404b15 (DA)

(a quote from Empedocles) "We see earth by earth, water by water, ether by godlike ether, fire by fire, love [στοργὴν] by love [στοργῇ], strife by gloomy strife."

611a12-13 (HA)

(tr. Thompson) In point of fact, the mare appears to be singularly prone by nature to maternal fondness [φιλόστοργον]; in proof whereof a barren mare will steal the foal from its dam and will tend it with all the solicitude [στέργουσι] of a mother...

613a7 (HA)

(tr. Thompson) As a general rule these birds have this manner of fondness [στέργουσιν] toward each other...

1000b8 (Metaphysics)

(a quote from Empedocles) "We see earth by earth, water by water, ether by godlike ether, fire by fire, love [στοργὴν] by love [στοργῇ], strife by gloomy strife."

1126b22-23 (EN)

It is the sort of person whose trait accords with the middle range that we consider when speaking of a decent friend, which entails affection [στέργειν]; but it differs from friendship in that it lacks feeling and affection [στέργειν]...

1156a15 (EN)

Those who love based on what's useful feel affection [στέργουσι] based on what is good for themselves...

1157a11 (EN)

Many lovers remain friends if, as a result of their intimacy, they become fond of [στέρξωσιν] each other's character, when they are similar in character.

1157a28 (EN)

People use the word 'friends' ... for those who are fond of [στέργοντας] of each other due to pleasure.

1161b18 (EN)

Parents are fond of [στέργουσι] their children as something that has their being from themselves.

1161b25 (EN)

Parents love for a longer time, since they are fond of [στέργουσιν] their children right from birth...

1162a12 (EN)

There are present in brotherly friendship the same things as in a fraternal association ... to the extent that they are more akin and have been fond of [στέργοντες] each other from birth...

1164a10 (EN)

... they were not fond of [ἔστεργον] each other but of attributes that belonged to them and were not lasting...

1168a2-8 (EN)

... poets have too much love [ὑπεραγαπῶσι] for their own poems, being fond of [στέργοντες] them like children.... The reason is that ... the work is in a certain way the maker at-work, so that he is fond of [στέργει] of the work because he is fond of being.

1168a22 (EN)

Everyone is more fond [στέργουσιν] of things that they have brought forth with effort, as those who have made money love it more than those who have inherited it...

1179b30 (EN)

A character must be present ahead of time that is inherent to thriving, being fond of [στέργον] what's beautiful and disliking what's shameful...

1180b6 (EN)

(about a father) ... his family members are already fond of [στέργοντες] him and obedient to him by nature.

1243b9 (EE)

(ironically?) As a result, each must love [στέργειν] his luck.

1328a16 (Politics)

(quoting a proverb) "Those who have loved [στέρξαντες] beyond measure hate beyond measure."

1336b33 (Politics)

We are always most fond [στέργομεν] of what we experience first.
