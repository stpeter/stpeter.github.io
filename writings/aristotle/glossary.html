An Aristotelian Glossary

by Peter Saint-Andre

Last Updated: 2023-11-21

  • Activity translates energeia (ἐνέργεια). This is perhaps the most important term in Aristotle's account of human life. Its core meaning is being engaged in working at a Task, which is why translator Joe Sachs rendered it as "being-at-work". Aristotle repeatedly points out that it's not good enough to be Thriving in your character and thinking, if that means merely having a Trait but not using and applying it in your day-to-day activities (after all, even someone who is asleep can have a trait). Life simply is activity.

  • Aliveness translates psuchē (ψυχή). Although psuchē was traditionally translated as "soul", for Aristotle it's what animates a living being, what makes it a living thing - i.e., its aliveness. Even plants have psuchē!

  • Awareness translates theōria (θεωρία). At root, the Activity that mostly starkly separates humans and other animals from plants and inanimate objects is their keen awareness of the world around them.

  • Balance translates mesotēs (μεσότης).

  • Beautifully Right translates kalos (καλός).

  • Beneficial translates agathos (ἀγαθός).

  • Capacity translates dunamis (δύναμις).

  • Character translates ēthos (ἦθος).

  • Commitment translates prohairesis (προαίρεσις).

  • Complete translates teleia (τελεία).

  • Completion translates entelecheia (ἐντελέχεια).

  • Correct translates orthos (ὀρθός).

  • Corruption translates mochthēria (μοχθηρία).

  • Craft translates technē (τέχνη).

  • Enjoyment translates hēdonē (ἡδονή). Every kind of Activity has its own Innate kind of enjoyment: the joys of eating, say, are quite different from the joys of beholding works of art. This is why the Standard of what's truly enjoyable is the Serious person of Complete Thriving in both Character and Thinking.

  • Fitting translates prepon (πρέπον).

  • Fulfillment translates eudaimonia (εὐδαιμονία).

  • Goal translates telos (τέλος).

  • Harmful translates kakos (κακός).

  • Humaneness translates epieikeia (ἐπιείκεια).

  • Identity translates to ti ēn einai (τὸ τί ἦν εἶναι). Literally, this key phrase means "what it is to be a thing". The Romans translated it as "essentia", which was modernized as "essence", thus leading to all sorts of confusion about independently existing essences. Aristotle's idea is much more straightforward, and is most reasonably Englished as "identity".

  • Impulsiveness translates akrasia (ἀκρασία).

  • Innate translates oikeios (οἰκεῖος). The Greek word oikeios derives from their word for home, and thus connotes that which is close to home, native as opposed to foreign, familiar as opposed to strange, inner as opposed to outer, and that which is innately bound up with the nature of a thing. A good example is the intimate tie between an Activity and the Enjoyment that completes it.

  • Insight translates nous (νοῦς).

  • Life translates zōē (ζωή).

  • Love translates philia (φιλία).

  • Moderation translates sōphrosunē (σωφροσύνη).

  • Nature translates phusis (φύσις).

  • Reaching translates orexis (ὄρεξις). All living organisms reach out into the world to achieve Goals such as metabolism, growth, and reproduction. For Aristotle, there are three kinds of reaching: Needs (evident even in pants), Wants (which emerge with Awareness and locomotion in animals), and Resolution (which results from Thinking, Reflection, and Commitment in humans).

  • Reaction translates pathos (πάθος).

  • Reflection translates bouleuesthai (βουλεύεσθαι).

  • Resolution translates boulēsis (βούλησις).

  • Sagacity translates sophia (σοφία).

  • Self-Indulgence translates akolasia (ἀκολασία).

  • Self-Restraint translates enkrateia (ἐγκράτεια).

  • Serious translates spoudaios (σπουδαῖος).

  • Social translates politikē (πολιτική).

  • Standard translates horos (ὅρος).

  • Taking Action translates praxis (πρᾶξις).

  • Task translates ergon (ἔργον).

  • Thinghood translates ousia (οὐσία).

  • Thinking translates logos (λόγος).

  • Thriving translates aretē (ἀρετή). This is likely the most controversial rendering of any term in this glossary. The traditional translation of aretē is "virtue"; more recently, some scholars have used the term "excellence" instead. Yet "virtue" sounds awfully Victorian or at least Christian, and "excellence" implies a competitive element that is missing in Aristotle. Instead this concept has its biological roots in the phenomena of Life and specifically of a human Way of Life. Following the lead of the Homeric verb ἀρετάω (to prosper, to fare well), I turn the infinitive of the Middle English verb "to thrive" into a noun, so that we can speak of, say, generosity as a Thriving of Character.

  • Trait translates hexis (ἕξις).

  • Understanding translates epistēmē (ἐπιστήμη).

  • Valuable translates haireta (αἱρετά).

  • Want translates thumos (θυμός).

  • Way of Being translates eidos (εἶδος).

  • Way of Life translates bios (βίος).

  • Wholeness translates autarkeia (αὐτάρκεια).

  • Wisdom translates phronēsis (φρόνησις).
