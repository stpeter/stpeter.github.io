ἐπιμέλεια in Aristotle

translated by Peter Saint-Andre

The following passages constitute occurrences of the term ἐπιμέλεια (and related words) in the works of Aristotle.

398a26 (DC)

... and others given care [ἐπιμεληταί] of the remaining tasks.

513a15 (HA)

... if someone takes a care [ἐπιμελές] about this subject.

563b10 (HA)

The majority of birds other than birds of prey, as has been said, also act in this manner, and after feeding their young take no further care [ἐπιμέλειαν] of them; but the crow is an exception.

581a25 (HA)

614b33 (HA)

626a33 (HA)

(the beekeeper is called ἐπιμελόμενος - "the one who takes care of them")

630b33 (HA)

(similarly with the keeper of elephants)

753a8 (GA)

952a22 (Problems)

... it is the care [ἐπιμελές] of those in the house to protect its contents ...

1026b4 (Metaphysics)

... regarding what is accidental, there can be no scientific treatment of it; this is confirmed by the fact that no science - practical, productive, or theoretical - takes a care [ἐπιμελές] about it...

1105b15 (EN)

(Just as sick people who listen with care [ἐπιμελῶς] to doctors but do none of the things they're told to do won't put their body in a good condition if they treat [θεραπευόμενοι] themselves that way, neither will people who merely talk put their soul in a good condition if they philosophize that way.)

1114a3-4 (EN)

(Sachs) ... and similarly in other cases in which people seem to be ignorant through carelessness [ἀμέλειαν], on the grounds that it is up to people themselves not to be ignorant, since they are in control of how much care [ἐπιμεληθῆναι] they take. But perhaps one is not the sort of person who takes care [ἐπιμεληθῆναι]....

1138b27 (EN)

(Sachs) ... "in all other pursuits [ἐπιμελείαις]" ... PSA: perhaps "areas of concern" or "matters of care"

1167b22-25 (EN)

(Sachs) ... "those who do favors wish for those who receive them to be there to return their favors, while making the return is not something the latter care [ἐπιμελές] about."

1179a22-29

One who is actively directed by insight and cultivates [θεραπεύων] this and so is best ordered would seem to be most loved by the gods; for if some care [ἐπιμέλεια] for things human comes from the gods, as is believed, then it would be reasonable for them to delight in what is best and what is most akin to them (and this is insight) and to act favorably in return especially toward those who cherish [ἀγαπῶντας] and honor this most, as such people care for [ἐπιμελουμένους] what they love and act rightly and beautifully.
