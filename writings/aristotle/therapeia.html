Aristotle on θεραπεία

translated by Peter Saint-Andre

The following passages constitute the occurrences of θεραπεία and related terms in the works of Aristotle.

545b32

The ewe will bear young up to eight years and, if beautifully tended, up to eleven years.

578a7

... those who tend to quadrupeds ...

585b25

... women who are unable to conceive except with treatment [διὰ θεραπείαν] ...

612b35

At the time of parturition in the female pigeon the sympathetic attentions [θεραπεία] of the male are extraordinary.

634a34

... a certain kind of person is in need of treatment ...

634b31

... that part of the body needs treatment ...

635b26

Hence women always need treatment [θεραπεία], in greater or lesser degree...

636a25

It can be treated [θεραπευτόν]...

760a3

... those occupied with tending [οἰ περὶ τὴν θεραπείαν] to these creatures ...

808b24 (Physiognomics)

Mania, it is generally allowed, is a condition of the soul, yet doctors cure it partly by administering purgative drugs to the body, partly by prescribing, besides these, certain courses of diet. Thus the result of proper treatments [θεραπείαις] of the body is that they succeed....

859b16 (Problems)

Why is it that the plague alone among diseases infects particularly persons who come into contact with those who are under treatment [θεραπευομένοις] for it?

981a23 (Metaphysics)

If, then, a man has the account [λόγος] without experience, and knows the universal but does not know the individual included in this, he will often fail in the treatment [θεραπείας]; for it is individual that is to be treated [θεραπευτόν].

1102a19 (EN)

... one who will treat the eyes must know about the whole body ...

1105b17 (EN)

(Just as sick people who listen with care [ἐπιμελῶς] to doctors but do none of the things they're told to do won't put their body in a good condition if they treat [θεραπευόμενοι] themselves that way, neither will people who merely talk put their soul in a good condition if they philosophize that way.) Note the analogy between treatment and philosophy, which is relevant to the meaning and import of θεραπεύειν at 1249b20.

1157a8 (EN)

Lover and beloved ... do not take pleasure in the same things, but the one in seeing the beloved and the other in receiving attentions [θεραπευόμενος] ... And sometimes, when the bloom of youth fades ... the attention [θεραπεία] is not forthcoming.

1167a18 (EN)

... someone is not a friend if he attends to [θεραπεύει] another because of some useful quality ...

1178b35 (EN)

... the body must be healthy and have food and other attention [θεραπείαν]...

1179a22-29

One who is actively directed by insight and cultivates [θεραπεύων] this and so is best ordered would seem to be most loved by the gods; for if some care [ἐπιμέλεια] for things human comes from the gods, as is believed, then it would be reasonable for them to delight in what is best and what is most akin to them (and this is insight) and to act favorably in return especially toward those who cherish [ἀγαπῶντας] and honor this most, as such people care for [ἐπιμελουμένους] what they love and act rightly and beautifully.

1181b3-4 (EN)

Even doctors do not seem to be made by a study of textbooks. Yet people try, at any rate, to state not only the treatments [θεραπεύματα], but also how particular classes of people can be cured and should be treated [θεραπεύειν]....

1249b20 (EE)

... tending [θεραπεύειν] and attending [θεωρεῖν] to god ...

1250b36 (VV)

To great-heartedness belong beautifully handling good fortune and bad fortune as well as honor and dishonor; admiring neither basking in luxury, nor being waited on [θεραπείαν], nor having free rein, nor besting others; and having a certain depth and grandeur in one's person.

1287a40 (Politics)

(Jowett modified) ... if a man suspected the physician of being in league with his enemies to destroy him for a bribe, he would rather receive treatment [θεραπείαν] by the book.

1329a32 (Politics)

(Jowett modified) Now since the body of the citizen is divided into two classes, the warriors and the councillors and it is beseeming that the service [θεραπείαν] of the Gods should be duly performed, and also a rest provided in their service for those who from age have given up active life, to the old men of these two classes should be assigned the duties of the priesthood.

1335b7 (Politics)

(Jowett modified) The disposition of an athlete is not conducive to the life of a citizen nor to health nor to the procreation of children, any more than is the disposition of being waited on [θεραπευτική] or being unaccustomed to hard work; what's conducive is a disposition which is in a mean between them.

1355b14 (Rhetoric)

(Roberts modified) It is possible to beautifully treat [θεραπεῦσαι καλῶς] even to those who can never enjoy sound health.

1436b32

... then you must wait on [θεραπευτέον] your audience ...

1442a14

(Forster modified) They must also wait on [θεραπεῦσαι] the jury by praising their justice and the intelligence which they bring to their task.
