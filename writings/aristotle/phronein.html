Aristotle on φρονεῖν

translated by Peter Saint-Andre

The following passages constitute the occurrences of the term φρονεῖν in the works of Aristotle.

247b11

(adapted from Sachs) It is by the calming [ἠρεμῆσαι] and standing still [στῆναι] of the thinking part [διάνοια] that we are said to understand [ἐπίστασθαι] and be minded [φρονεῖν].

455b23

Being awake [ἐγρήγορσις] is a completion [τέλος], since being aware [αἰσθάνεσθαι] or being minded [φρονεῖν] is the goal for all beings to which either of these appertains...

686a29

Of all animals mankind alone stands erect, in accordance with our god-like [θεία] nature and thinghood [οὐσία]. For it is the task [ἔργον] of the god-like to think [νοεῖν] and be minded [φρονεῖν]."

But we should not follow those who say that, being human, we should be minded in a human way and, being mortal, we should be minded in a mortal way.

1313b8

... they would get in the habit of thinking small [φρονεῖν ἂν ἐθίζοιντο μικρὸν] and as a result of always living like slaves.

1314a16

... tyranny aims at three things; one is for its subjects to think small [μικρὰ φρονεῖν], since a small-souled person [μικρόψυχος] would not plot against anyone.

1314a29

... one may trace every tyrannical measure back to these three underlying purposes: making the people not trust each other, making them have no power, and making them think small [μικρὸν φρονῶσιν].
