ἐπαμφοτερίζω in Aristotle

translated by Peter Saint-Andre

The following passages constitute occurrences of the term ἐπαμφοτερίζω in the works of Aristotle.

  • Plato Phaedrus 257b ἐπαμφοτερίζῃ = Phaedrus will not "fluctuate" (or "hesitate"?) between two ways
  • Plato Republic 479b ἐπαμφοτερίζουσιν = objects that "have a double sense"
  • 205a29 ἐπαμφοτερίζει = the place of water and air "is ambiguous" between the up and down directions
  • 259a25 ἐπαμφοτερίζοντα = things that are sometimes in motion and sometimes at rest "fluctuate/waver" between the two principles
  • 328b9 ἐπαμφοτερίζει = attempting to combine materials like tin and bronze shows that some things are hesitant [ψελλίζεται] and "ambivalent" towards each other
  • 488a1-7 see HA and Depew
  • 498a18 ἐπαμφοτερίζει = the legs of crocodiles and lizards move in an "intermixed" manner
  • 499b12 ἐπαμφοτερίζει = the genos of swine "dualize" since some are cloven-footed and some are not
  • 499b21 ditto
  • 502a16 ἐπαμφοτερίζει = some animals "dualize" between humans and quadrupeds, such as the ape, monkey, and baboon
  • 511a25 ἐπαμφοτερίζει = animals that are externally viviparous and internally oviparous "dualize"
  • 529b24 ἐπαμφοτερίζειν = the hermit crab seems to "dualize" between crustaceans and testaceans
  • 566b27 ἐπαμφοτεριζὀντων = the seal "wavers" between being a land animal and a marine animal
  • 584b28 ἐπαμφοτερίζει = humans "fluctuate" between having one baby and more than one
  • 589a21 ἐπαμφοτερίζειν = various animals "are ambivalent" between land and water in whether they inhale air or take in water, in where they feed, etc.
  • 590a8 ditto
  • 598a15 ἐπαμφοτερίζουσιν = fishes that "fluctuate" between shallow and deep water
  • 602a17 ditto
  • 669a9 ἐπαμφοτερίζει = more animals that "are ambivalent" between land and water
  • 669b15 ἐπαμφοτερίζειν = the liver and spleen "are ambiguous" between one organ and two
  • 681b1 ἐπαμφοτερίζει = anemones "are ambiguous" between plant and animal
  • 689b32 ἐπαμφοτερίζειν = the ape "is ambiguous" between humans and quadrupeds in form
  • 697b1 ἐπαμφοτερίζειν = seals and bats "are ambivalent", the former between land and water and the latter between land and air
  • 772b1 ἐπαμφοτερίζει = humans "are ambivalent" between three classes: animals that have one young, that have two young, and that have multiple young
  • 777a16 ἐπαμφοτερίζειν = nature "hesitates" to provide both milk and menses at the same time
  • 941b30 ἐπαμφοτερίζειν = during the period of Orion the winds are unsettled because the seasons are "wavering" or "fluctuating" one into the other
  • 1197a31 ἐπαμφοτερίζομεν = ὑπόληψις (weighing alternative accounts) is that whereby we "waver" about whether something is one way or another
  • 1332b2 ἐπαμφοτερίζοντα = some human qualities "waver" between worse and better depending on a person's ἤθη / character traits
  • 1337b23 ἐπαμφοτερίζουσιν = educational subjects (e.g., music) "teeter" between freehearted and servile depending on the aim
