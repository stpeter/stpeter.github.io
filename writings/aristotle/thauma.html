θαῦμα / θαυμάζειν in Aristotle

translated by Peter Saint-Andre

The following passages constitute occurrences of the terms θαῦμα and θαυμάζειν in the works of Aristotle.

  • 126b15ff δοκεῖ γὰρ ἡ ἔκπληξις θαυμασιότης εἶναι ὑπερβάλλουσα = "astonishment (or surprise) seems to be an excess of wonder"
  • 271b15 τὸ θαυμαστήν = it should not be a matter of "amazement" to consider the idea that an infinite body exists
  • 317b18 θαυμαστήν = there is an "amazing" impasse involved in determining whether there can be unqualified coming-to-be
  • 342a5 θαυμαστή = the flame moves "amazingly" fast
  • 402a3 θαυμασιωτέρων = the "greater wondrousness" of knowledge about the soul
  • 571b16 θαυμαστάς = boars fight with each other "amazingly"
  • 609a15 θαυμάζειν = small birds that flutter around an owl are said to "be amazed" by him
  • 633a8 ἐθαυμαστώθη = the cooing of the ring-dove in winter "amazes" the experts
  • 847a11** θαυμάζεται = "we are amazed" by phenomena of which we don't know the cause
  • 847b17ff** θαυμασιωτέρου = a lesser "marvel" being caused by a greater one
  • 848a11** θαυμάτων = the circle is the cause of a "marvel"
  • 913a22 θαυμασιωτέρον = "more amazing"
  • 917a8 θαυματοποιός = "conjurer" or "magician" ("someone who produces amazement")
  • 979b7** θαυμάστιον = "amazing" to say that what is not is in fact is
  • 982b12 θαυμάζειν = it is because of "wondering" that people began to philosophize
  • 983a14 θαυμάσειεν = all people begin by "wondering"
  • 1063a36 θαυμαστόν = if we are always changing and never remain the same, what "womder" is it if things never appear the same?
  • 1082b21 θαυμαστόν = "amazing"
  • 1095a26 θαυμάζουσιν = ignorant people "being astonished" at someone who proclaims a big thing beyond their comprehension
  • 1125a2 θαυμαστικός = the great-hearted person is not "given to amazement"
  • 1239a26ff θαυμάζεσθαι ἐν ὑπεροχῇ = "being an object of amazement involves superiority"
  • 1341a11 θαυμάσια = "marvels"
  • 1371a32 τὸ μανθάνειν καὶ τὸ θαυμάζειν ἡδὺ = "learning and wondering are pleasant"
  • 1384a29 = those whose opinion matters to us are "those who admire us [θαυμαζόντων], those whom we admire [θαυμάζει], those by whom we wish to be admired [βούλεται θαυμάζεσθαι]"
  • 1384b37 θαυμασταὶ γάρ οἱ ζηλωταί = "jealousy involves admiration"
  • 1404b11-12 θαυμασταὶ γὰρ τῶν ἀπόντων εἰσίν, ἡδὺ δὲ τὸ θαυμαστόν = "what amazes is pleasing, and this is what's striking"
  • 1460a12 δεῖ μὲν οῦν έν ταῖς τραγῳδίαις ποιεῖν τὸ θαυμαστόν = "the wondrous is a necessity in drama"
