ἐπάλλαξις in Aristotle

translated by Peter Saint-Andre

The following passages constitute occurrences of the term ἐπάλλαξις in the works of Aristotle.

  • Plato Sophist 240c διὰ τῆς ἐπαλλάξεως = "by this confusion/intermixture" of a sophist's arguments
  • 79b7 μὴ ἐπαλλάτουσιν ἀλλήλαις = chains of premises that are "not overlapping each other"
  • 387a12 ἐπαλλάξει = "interlocking" like parts of a chain
  • 460b20 ἐπαλλάξει = one object seems to be two when one's fingers are "crossed"
  • 501a18ff ἐπαλλάττει = saw-toothed teeth that "interlock"
  • 526a2 ἐπαλλάττοντα = "overlapping" of fins on a crayfish
  • 661b18-21 ἐπαλλάττοντας = sharp-pointed teeth that are "interlocking"
  • 720b10 ἐπαλλάξῃ = tails that "fit into each other" or "interlock"
  • 732b15 ἐπάλλαξις = "cross-division" of animal kinds
  • 733a27 ἐπαλλάττουςι = "overlap" between insects that produce a grub and animals that lay ‘imperfect' eggs
  • 769b34-36 ἐπαλλάττειν = in the case of monstrosities, the parts of an embryo grow together and are "confused/intermixed"
  • 770b6 ἐπαλλάττει = the error of a monstrosity is "intermixed" into animals of such a nature
  • 774b17 ἐπαλλάττει = there is "overlap" because pigs have many and complete young like many-toed animals, even though they are cloven-footed or solid-hoofed
  • 898a15 ἐπαλλαττόντων = monstrosities come about when the semen is "confused"
  • 939a13 ἐπαλλάτουσι = the pores of pumice-stone are "overlapping"
  • 958b14 ἐπαλλάξεως = one object seems to be two when one's fingers are "crossed"
  • 959a16 ditto
  • 1011a33 ditto
  • 1255a13 ἐπαλλάττειν = opinions about whether slavery is by nature or convention are "confused/intermixed"
  • 1257b36 ἐπαλλάττει = the acquisition of natural wealth and the amassing of excessive wealth "overlap" because the tool is the same but the use (i.e., the aim) is different
  • 1295a9 ἐπαλλάττειν = two forms of tyranny are according to law and therefore "overlap" with kingship
  • 1317a2 ἐπαλλάττειν = the modes of government "overlap" so that aristocracies have oligarchical characteristics and constitutional governments have democratic characteristics
