No One Wiser

An Intellectual Biography of Socrates

by Peter Saint-Andre

After I finish my books about Aristotle and Pyrrho, I'd like to write a brief biography of Socrates.

At the moment I am thinking that the book will consist of of twelve chapters, one for each of the eleven trilogies of Plato's Socratic dialogues (in dramatic order) plus a twelfth chapter on Socrates' legacy:

  1. Before the War: Parmenides, Protagoras, First Alcibiades
  2. Potidaea and Delium: Charmides, Republic, Laches
  3. A Mythical Interlude: Timaeus, Critias, Minos
  4. The Peace of Nicias: Lysis, Hippias Major, Hippias Minor
  5. The Sicilian Expedition: Symposium, Hipparchus, Ion
  6. The Expulsion from Thurii: Euthydemus, Erastai, Clitophon
  7. Warâ€™s End: Theages, Second Alcibiades, Gorgias
  8. After the War: Phaedrus, Menexenus, Meno
  9. Indictment: Theaetetus, Euthyphro, Cratylus
  10. Trial: Sophist, Statesman, Apology
  11. Imprisonment and Execution: Philebus, Crito, Phaedo
  12. Legacy: The Case Against Socrates; A Further Defense; An Examined Life

It's possible that each chapter might be a modern dialogue, but I have yet to settle on the exact format.

As always with projects of this sort, I'm keeping notes and maintaining a reading list.
